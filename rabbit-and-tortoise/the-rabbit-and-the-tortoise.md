The Rabbit and the Tortoise

(A short story for kids)

Once upon a time, in a beautiful forest, there lived a rabbit named Rocky and a tortoise named Tommy.

Rocky was the fastest animal in the forest. He loved to run and often boasted about his speed.

One day, Rocky saw Tommy slowly walking along the forest path.

"Tommy, why do you walk so slowly?" Rocky laughed. "You will never reach anywhere on time!"

Tommy smiled and replied, "Slow and steady can also win the race."

The rabbit burst into laughter. "You? Win a race against me? That's impossible!"

Hearing this, the other animals gathered around. They suggested a race between Rocky and Tommy.

The next morning, all the animals assembled near the starting line.

"Ready... Set... Go!" shouted the fox.

Rocky dashed ahead like the wind. Within minutes, he was far ahead of Tommy.

Looking back, Rocky saw Tommy still moving slowly.

"He is so far behind," Rocky said confidently. "I have plenty of time to rest."

Seeing a shady tree nearby, Rocky lay down and quickly fell asleep.

Meanwhile, Tommy kept moving forward—slowly but without stopping.

Step by step, Tommy passed the sleeping rabbit and continued toward the finish line.

After some time, Rocky woke up.

"Oh no!" he cried. "I must hurry!"

Rocky ran as fast as he could, but it was too late.

Tommy had already crossed the finish line.

All the animals cheered loudly.

Rocky felt embarrassed and congratulated Tommy.

Tommy smiled and said, "Success comes to those who keep going and never give up."

Moral of the Story:

Slow and steady wins the race.
